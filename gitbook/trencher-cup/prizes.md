# Prize Distribution

## The Reward Vault

The Trencher Cup prize pool comes from the **Reward Vault** — a fund that accumulates automatically from $WEB3ME token activity.

### How the Vault Fills

**69% of all $WEB3ME creator fees** flow directly into the Reward Vault. This is hardcoded — not a promise, not a governance vote. Every single $WEB3ME transaction grows the pot.

```
$WEB3ME Transaction
    │
    ↓
Creator Fee
    │
    ├── 69% → REWARD VAULT
    │         ├── Trencher Cup prizes
    │         └── Weekly leaderboard payouts
    │
    └── 31% → Operations & development
```

### Why This Matters

Most crypto competitions use:
- VC money (runs out)
- Airdrop allocations (inflationary)
- Marketing budgets (not sustainable)

The Reward Vault is **revenue-funded**. As long as people trade $WEB3ME, the vault grows. More volume = bigger prizes. It's a flywheel, not a one-time event.

## Trencher Cup Prize Split

Prizes are distributed to the top finishers based on their final placement:

| Placement | Prize Share |
|-----------|------------|
| Champion | 50% |
| Runner-up | 25% |
| 3rd place | 15% |
| 4th place | 10% |

**Example:** If the vault has accumulated 100 SOL for a season:
- Champion: 50 SOL
- Runner-up: 25 SOL
- 3rd: 15 SOL
- 4th: 10 SOL

## Weekly Leaderboard Payouts

Between Trencher Cup seasons, the vault also funds **weekly leaderboard distributions**:

- Top 10 traders on the rolling 7-day leaderboard receive weekly payouts
- Distribution is weighted by rank (1st gets the largest share)
- Payouts are sent directly to the trader's linked wallet
- Rankings are based on on-chain verified PnL

This ensures there's always something to compete for — even between Cup seasons.

## Claiming Prizes

- Prizes are distributed in **SOL** (or $WEB3ME — TBD based on community preference)
- Sent directly to the winner's primary linked wallet
- No claiming process — prizes are distributed automatically after the final
- Transaction is verifiable on-chain
